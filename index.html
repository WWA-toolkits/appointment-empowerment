🎯 Define Your Purpose
▼

Knowledge is Power: Patients who prepare for appointments report higher satisfaction. Your health journey is important.

📋 Your Preparation Checklist
▼
  • Complete medication list: Include prescriptions, vitamins, supplements, and herbal remedies.
  • Health tracking data: Blood pressure logs, symptom diary, etc.
  • Family health history.
  • Recent test results or reports.
  • Support person or advocate (if desired).
  • Insurance information & ID.

💬 Questions That Get Clear Answers
▼

Trust Your Instincts: If something doesn't feel right, speak up. Your concerns are valid and deserve respect.

  • "What are all my options?" (Not just what they recommend).
  • "What happens if I wait or do nothing?".
  • "How will we know if the treatment is working?".
  • "What are the risks and side effects?".
  • "Can you explain that in everyday language?".
  • "How does this relate to my family history?".
💡 Real-Time Advocacy Tips
▼

If You Feel Dismissed: "I need you to document in my chart that you're choosing not to run this test/address this concern."


If You're Interrupted: "I wasn't finished explaining my symptoms. This is important for my care."


If You Need Time: "I need a moment to think about this" or "I'd like to discuss this with my family first."

📝 Capture the Details While Fresh
▼

Your Visit Matters Beyond Today: Documenting your experience helps track patterns and advocate for better care in the future.

⚖️ You Have The Right To:
▼

Healthcare Equity Matters: You have the right to culturally competent care, to be treated with dignity and respect, and to have your concerns taken seriously.

  • Understand everything being done to your body.
  • Say "no" or "not yet" to any treatment.
  • Get a second opinion without judgment.
  • Have a support person present.
  • Request a different provider if you're uncomfortable.
  • Have your pain and symptoms taken seriously.
  • Receive culturally competent care.
  • Access interpretation services if needed.
  • See your medical records anytime.
